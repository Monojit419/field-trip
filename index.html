About Me

I am Monojit Mandal, an undergraduate student at IISER Bhopal with a strong academic interest in Earth and Environmental Sciences, with a primary focus on geology and geochemistry.

I am particularly drawn to understanding the processes that govern Earth's interior — including crust–mantle interactions, mantle plume activity, and the formation and evolution of Large Igneous Provinces (LIPs). My training in mineralogy, petrology, and physical chemistry has equipped me with a geochemical lens through which I interpret geological systems.

I am especially interested in the use of geochemical proxies and isotopic signatures to trace magma sources, interpret tectonic environments, and reconstruct Earth's deep-time history. Beyond the subsurface, I connect geological processes with broader environmental systems — including geochemical cycling and lithosphere–surface interactions.

I approach problems with analytical rigour and a quantitative mindset, and I am actively seeking research and field-based opportunities in geochemistry, petrology, and environmental Earth systems.
